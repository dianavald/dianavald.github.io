Otto


Otto is the only connected service for people who want to improve and maintain their life quality that provides the user with suggestions, and that takes spontaneous decisions in order to prevent the stress level from becoming too high while managing every day’s daily chores.

Otto helps the user by tracking his activity and by understanding its behaviours. Artificial intelligence is the key point for learning the user’s behaviour and to track its activity in a proactive way.

We aim to create a service which will help people in recognising patterns and events that lead to stress in order to prevent and manage it in a non invasive and not direct way.

Our customers will be people from 16 to the retirement age, which face an everyday stressful life. They can be students, workers or people facing other situations.

We realize that many persons can’t recognise what leads them to stress, so they can’t face the situation in a proper way. Also ignorance about how to deal with stress can lead to unproductive advices or unproductive actions.

We wanted our application interface to be as immediate as possible, in or¬der to relief any possible “application learning” stress.

In order to create the assistant and to get to know the user, we defined a short tutorial, where the basics of the interface are explained and the assistant is personalised.

We then defined a custom main page (Homepage), used to interact and to chat with the assistant (the AI rep¬resentation), and a standard OS GUI interface for the rest of the applica¬tion.

A project by Edy Radice, Minbau Liao and Diana Valdes.
